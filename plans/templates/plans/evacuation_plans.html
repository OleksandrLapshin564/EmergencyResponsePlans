Evacuation Plans

An evacuation plan is a structured strategy that ensures the safe and orderly movement of people away from dangerous or potentially dangerous areas. Having a clear evacuation plan saves lives during emergencies such as fires, earthquakes, floods, or industrial accidents.

Key Elements of an Evacuation Plan:

  • Evacuation Routes: Identify primary and secondary routes to safe locations.
  • Assembly Points: Establish designated meeting areas for accountability.
  • Special Needs: Consider elderly, children, or disabled persons when planning.
  • Communication: Define how to alert and inform people during evacuation.
  • Practice: Conduct regular evacuation drills to test readiness.

A well-prepared evacuation plan reduces confusion and ensures a quicker, safer response during emergencies.
