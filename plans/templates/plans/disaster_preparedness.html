Disaster Preparedness

Disaster preparedness involves planning and taking proactive measures to protect lives and property before an emergency occurs. Effective preparedness reduces the impact of disasters such as floods, fires, earthquakes, and industrial accidents.

Key Steps in Disaster Preparedness:

  • Risk Assessment: Identify potential hazards in your area.
  • Emergency Plan: Create a plan including evacuation routes, meeting points, and communication channels.
  • Emergency Kit: Stock essential items: water, non-perishable food, first aid kit, flashlight, batteries, and important documents.
  • Training and Drills: Practice safety drills for family, employees, or community members.
  • Stay Informed: Monitor news, weather alerts, and official emergency notifications.

Being prepared helps reduce panic and ensures a more organized response when disaster strikes.
